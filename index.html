QR Check-In System Flow

How a single family scan triggers the full SafeBoard response chain, from detection to boarding.

01
Family Arrives at Priority Zone
Parent with stroller, wagon, or child enters the green Family Priority Zone on the platform. The designated zone separates family boarding from the general queue.
Platform Zone Stroller Parking
02
QR Code Scan — Direct Family Input
Parent scans the QR kiosk. The scan captures: number of children, equipment type (stroller/wagon), destination. A unique session ID is generated and logged in real-time.
QR Check-In Session ID Created Data Captured
03
AI Camera + Sensor Validation
AI overhead cameras cross-validate the QR signal — confirming stroller presence, child detection, and crowd density. QR + AI data are fused to compute a precise demand score.
AI Detection Sensor Fusion Demand Score
04
Dynamic Mode Selected
Based on the combined demand score, the system selects one of three modes: Low (1–2 families → small section), Medium (3–5 families → half coach), High (6+ families → full priority coach).
Low Demand Medium Demand High Demand
05
Train Coach Space Allocated
The train receives the allocation command: foldable seats deploy, stroller bays are cleared, priority seating is reserved with green indicators. The flexible section adapts to match the selected mode.
Coach Config Foldable Seats Stroller Bay
05a
Pre-Positioning While Train Is Still En Route NEW IN v2
This is the key fix for schedule impact. While the train is still approaching (typically 1–4 minutes away on a 7-minute headway), staff walk to the correct door, seats begin folding, and the stroller bay clears — all before arrival. This is what allows the extra door-hold to shrink to just +0–15s on top of the standard 45s default.
Lead-Time Prep Staff Pre-Positioned
06
Capped Door Hold + Boarding Lane Activated
On top of the standard 45s default, the train door stay-open time extends by a tiered, capped amount — +0s at low demand, up to +15s at high demand, never more. Because pre-positioning already happened during the wait, this short hold is enough. The Family Boarding Lane activates with green lighting. Station staff receive a push notification to assist.
+0 to +15s (Capped) Lane Activated Staff Alerted
07
Station Display Updated
Platform screens and train door displays update in real-time: boarding instructions, active mode, car number, door hold countdown. All families in the queue receive confirmation of their boarding slot.
Display Live Notification Sent
✓
Family Boards Safely — No Rushing
With extra door time, dedicated lane, reserved space, and staff support, the family boards safely with stroller/wagon. The system returns to monitoring mode and re-evaluates demand for the next train.
Safe Boarding Stress Reduced System Resets

What Changed in v2

After our presentation, our lecturer raised a real operational concern about schedule impact. Here's how we fixed it.

Lecturer Feedback
"A flat +20–30 second door-hold at every station will compound across an LRT line. With only about 7 minutes between trains, this could create severe cumulative delay and disrupt arrival timing system-wide."

1
Tiered, Capped Hold
Door-hold scales with real, confirmed demand instead of being flat — +0s at low demand, +8s at medium, capped at +15s maximum even at peak. This is on top of the standard 45s default door time.
2
QR Pre-Positioning
Families check in while still waiting on the platform — before the train arrives. That lead time lets staff and seats get ready in advance, so the hold itself can be much shorter.
3
Dynamic Recovery
Any time used at one station is clawed back at the next quiet station — instead of permanently padding the whole schedule.
≈ 0 sec
Net schedule impact across a full line run, in typical demand conditions — down from +2.5 minutes in Version 1.
